A search for semi-visible decays of the SM Higgs boson into photon and \ETmiss\ is conducted, exploiting a new topological trigger to target, for the first time, the ggF Higgs production channel and a previously unexplored phase space down to \ETmiss>100 GeV and \pTy > 50 GeV. The analysis is performed using Run-3 $pp$ collision data at $\sqrt{s}=13.6$ TeV, collected by the ATLAS experiment in 2023 and 2024, corresponding to an integrated luminosity of $134.6~\ifb$. 

The effect of PV misidentification, due to low track activity, is strongly mitigated by training a BDT to suppress them. Novel data driven techniques are developed to model the background arising from electrons and jets misidentified as photons, while the normalization of the background processes with a genuine photon is obtained using MC simulations constrained by data in dedicated control regions.  

No excess over the SM expectation is observed, and the results are interpreted in terms of a $H\to\gamma\gammad$ decay. The analysis sets the best exclusion limit to date on the \br, with an observed (expected) upper limit  of $1.10\%$ ($0.94\%$) at 95\% CL, superseding the current best limits of 1.3\% (1.5\%) from the ATLAS Run-2 combination~\cite{ATLAS:2024cju}. Combining this analysis with the Run-2 results, an observed (expected) upper limit of 0.82\% (0.77\%) at 95\% CL is achieved.
