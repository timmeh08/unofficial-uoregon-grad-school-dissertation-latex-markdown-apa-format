The $H\to\gamma\gamma_d$ signal is characterised by the presence of a single photon and \ETmiss. Though the $z$-component of the \gammad\, momentum escapes the detector, the \mT\ results in a broad peak around the Higgs boson mass. In the ggF channel, the final state contains no leptons or $b$-tagged jets, while hadronic jets may originate from initial- or final-state radiation.
The same final state also covers the VBF channel and the VH production with hadronic decay of the $V$ bosons. %The SM Higgs boson mass of 125 GeV leads to a photon with an average photon energy, \( E_\gamma = m_H/2 \) and similar \ETmiss\, in the Higgs center-of-mass frame due to the escaping \gammad. 

Due to the limitations imposed by the trigger thresholds on \ETmiss\ and photon \pTy\ (Section \ref{sec:trigger}), optimised as a compromise between signal acceptance and trigger rate, the accepted signal events are mostly constituted by Higgs bosons recoiling against jets, leading to a boosted event topology. 

This analysis phase space is significantly affected by background from jets and electrons misidentified as photons (hereafter referred to as \textit{jet} or \textit{electron faking photon}), as well as by events reconstructed with large \ETmiss, due to mismeasurement or misreconstruction of physics objects (referred to as \textit{fake \ETmiss}).
In addition, the low track activity leads to a non-negligible probability of PV misidentification, further enhancing the contribution from fake 
\ETmiss\ due to the removal of HS jets incorrectly classified as PU.
The SM background events with similar signatures can be classified in the following categories:
