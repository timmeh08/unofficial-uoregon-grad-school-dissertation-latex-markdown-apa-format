A search for semi-visible decays of the SM Higgs boson into photon and \ETmiss\ is conducted, exploiting a new topological trigger to target, for the first time, the ggF Higgs production channel and a previously unexplored phase space with \ETmiss>100 GeV and \pTy > 50 GeV. The analysis is performed using Run-3 $pp$ collision data at $\sqrt{s}=13.6$ TeV, collected by the ATLAS experiment in 2023 and 2024, corresponding to an integrated luminosity of $134.6~\ifb$. 

The effect of PV misidentification due to low track activity is strongly mitigated by training a BDT to suppress such kind of events. Data driven techniques are developed to model the background arising from electron or jet misidentified as photons, while the normalization of the background with a genuine photon is obtained using MC simulations constrained by data in dedicated control regions.  

No excess over SM expectation is observed, and the results are interpreted in terms of a $H\to\gamma\gammad$ decay. The analysis provides the best exclusion limit at present on the $BR(H\to\gamma\gammad)$, with an observed (expected) upper limit  of $1.16\%$ ($0.92^{+0.46}_{-0.28}\%$) at 95\% CL. Combined with Run-2 results, the observed (expected) upper limit is $0.85\%$ ($0.78^{+0.35}_{-0.22}\%$) at 95\% CL, improving by a factor of 2 the previous best limit on this decay channel.
