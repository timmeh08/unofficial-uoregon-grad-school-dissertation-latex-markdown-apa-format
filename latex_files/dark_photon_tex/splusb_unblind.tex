The SR is then unblinded. The same signal-plus-background fit is performed simultaneously in the CRs and in the SR, this time using real data in the SR. The results of the corresponding background-only fit are largely consistent, and are reported for a comparison in Appendix ~\ref{sec:bonly-unblind-appendix}.\\
Figures~\ref{fig:pre_plots_unblind} and ~\ref{fig:post_plots_unblind} show background and signal distributions before and after the fit in SR and in CRs, and the corresponding event yields are summarized in Tables~\ref{tab:yields_pre_syst_unblind} and~\ref{tab:yields_post_syst_unblind}. 
The fitted values of the k-factors and their related statistical uncertainties are shown in Figure~\ref{fig:kfactors_splusb_unblind}. The values differ slightly from the blind fit, but remain consistent and close to unity, with similar uncertainties as in the blind case. Also the signal strength remains consistent with 0: $BR(H\to\gamma\gamma_D)=0.0\pm 0.0055$, therefore no significant excess is observed in data.\\
