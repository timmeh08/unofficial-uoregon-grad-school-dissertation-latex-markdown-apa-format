A set of pre-selections is applied to remove events with poor data quality, either due to instrumental limitations such as known detector defects or problematic lumiblocks, or due to large contamination from non-collision background such as cosmic rays or beam-induced background. 
These pre-selections follow the Data Preparation recommendations: 
\begin{itemize}
	\item{Only events in the GRL are retained, to remove events collected in lumiblocks with not fully functional detector;}	
	\item{Remove events collected in data-taking periods affected by LAr noise burst, Tile corrupted events, single SCT upsets, or incomplete events due to TTC restart;}
	\item{Jet cleaning is applied to remove jets arising from non-collision background. Events with any $LooseBad$ jet, overlapping with neither leptons nor photons, with calibrated $p_{\mathrm{T}}>20$ GeV are rejected;}
    \item{At least one primary vertex must be reconstructed, with at least two associated good-quality tracks with $p_{\mathrm{T}}>400$ MeV and $|\eta|<2.5$;}
\end{itemize}


In addition to the quality selections, leptons and b-jets are vetoed. Due to the fact that most of the signal events selected at trigger level are characterized by the presence of ISR or FSR jets boosting the Higgs boson, at most 3 central jets are accepted. This is found to be the smallest jet multiplicity allowing to retain a good signal acceptance (less than 10\% signal loss).  Offline thresholds on the trigger variables are applied, with values chosen as a compromise between acceptance and trigger efficiency. 
\begin{itemize}
	\item{No baseline lepton;}
	\item{No b-jet;}
	\item{At most 3 central jets;}
	\item{$\ETmiss>100$ GeV;}
	\item{$\pTy>50$ GeV;}
	\item{$\mT>80$ GeV;}
\end{itemize}

The selection on $\pTy$ and $\mT>80$ GeV are consistent with the trigger thresholds, taking advantage of the extremely fast turn-on (see Ref.~\cite{TRIG-2018-05} \href{Fig.04}{https://atlas.web.cern.ch/Atlas/GROUPS/PHYSICS/PAPERS/TRIG-2018-05/fig\_04.png}), while a more conservative approach is used for the offline cut on $\ETmiss$. 
Since these offline selections do not allow to reach a 100\% trigger efficiency, the agreement in trigger efficiency between MC and data is investigated as described in section \ref{sec:triggerSF}, to correct for possible mismodelling in MC simulations. \\
