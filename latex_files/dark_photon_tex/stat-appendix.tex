Figure \ref{fig:Norm_kfactors} and Figure \ref{fig:Shape_kfactors} shows the k-factors values obtained from the fit using, respectivly, normalization and shape factors in OneMuCR. 
The k-factors obtained using a normalization factor in OneMuCR are closer to unity, while the k-factors obtained using a shape factor in OneMuCR are close to one expected for the second bin where the shape factor is more close to 0.9. 
In conclusion, using shape factors in OneMuCR seem to absorb possible corrections due to systematic variations and statistical uncertainties of $W\gamma$ and \jfakey. These factors adjust the distribution of the $W\gamma$ process to match the data without any physical prior, thus giving the fit too much freedom.
