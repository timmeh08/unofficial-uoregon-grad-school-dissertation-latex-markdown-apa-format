The missing transverse momentum $\ETmiss$ , originating from the unbalanced momentum in the transverse plane, is defined as the negative vectorial sum of the transverse momenta of all baseline
electrons, muons, photons, PFlow jets, and of the “soft term”, which is estimated from tracks associated with the primary vertex, but not matched to any of those objects. The Tight WP for jets is used, building the jet term only from jets with $\pt > 20$ GeV, with a tight requirement on the NNJVT score.\\
An alternative, \ETmiss definition, with no PU suppressione ($\ETmissNoJVT$), is also used in this analysis, to reduce the contribution from events with misidentified primary vertex. In $\ETmissNoJVT$, no JVT requirement is applied to the jets entering the jet term, which will therefore include jets from any reconstructed vertex. For events with wrong PV identification, this will allow to retain the hard-scatter jets that would be otherwise rejected by the JVT selection.
